Experimental Study on LMS Usability in Virtual Education

Research Overview

  • This paper explores whether Learning Management Systems (LMS) truly enhance virtual learning experiences for students.
  • An experimental methodology was applied using a control and an experiment group among MSIT students at University of Cincinnati.
  • Both groups were surveyed using a perceived usefulness (PU) questionnaire before and after sharing a learning article with only the experiment group.
  • Quantitative analysis showed a dramatic rise in mean score from 8 to 17 in the experiment group, along with reduced standard deviation, validating consistent positive responses post-intervention.
  • Findings conclude LMS platforms improve accessibility, interaction, and learning satisfaction, replicating the in-person classroom experience.
