This study explores the real-world impact of Learning Management Systems (LMS) on virtual learning environments. Through a rigorous Systematic Literature Review (SLR), the research identifies how LMS platforms improve learning experiences through features like centralized content management, scalability, academic progress tracking, and enhanced teacher-student collaboration. The review process involved applying inclusion/exclusion criteria to over 350 academic sources from ACM and IEEE digital libraries, resulting in distilled insights from 10 high-quality peer-reviewed papers.

Survey on Student Satisfaction with Virtual Learning Environments

This project investigates students' satisfaction levels with virtual learning platforms using a quantitative survey approach. Conducted among MSIT students, the research applied descriptive statistical analysis on responses gathered through a Google Form. Findings revealed that 71% of students felt comfortable using virtual platforms like LMS and Zoom, citing convenience, resilience, and learning flexibility. However, a minority expressed dissatisfaction due to missing human interaction and traditional classroom dynamics.

Case Study

Experiment on Usability of LMS for Virtual Learning

This research investigates how effective Learning Management Systems (LMS) are in enhancing students’ experience in virtual learning. Using a controlled experiment with two student groups, it compares perceived usefulness before and after exposure to academic resources on LMS. A quantitative analysis of survey data showed a significant increase in perceived usefulness within the experiment group, supported by a drop in standard deviation and standard error, proving consistency in responses. The study concludes that LMS platforms meaningfully improve usability, engagement, and productivity in virtual learning settings.
